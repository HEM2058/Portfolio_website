Geospatial Technology in Present World

Introduction: Geospatial Technology is an emerging field of study which includes Geographic Information System(GPS), Remote Sensing, Global Navigation Satellite System(GNSS), Geodesy, Surveying, Lidar, radar, Earth observation etc. Present Condition: Currently, The use of location-based services is increasing day by day, so that the use of navigation and satellite positioning is becoming major. The growing technology offers geospatial features to become in digital format so that geospatial data is more easily accessible to people. The Cadastre system of every country is switching to digital Cadastre, resulting in accuracy, reliability, less tediousness etc for accessing, manipulating, management, administrating both the spatial(Vector and Raster) and non-spatial data. Technology also allows us to measure the complicated shape and size of the earth and celestial bodies in a more precise way. The high resolution satellite imaginary and the UAV captured imaginary are used for remote sensing and surveying. GIS helps us to make decisions for our Disaster Risk Reduction and engineering projects. Moreover, GNSS and GPS are being used to coordinate determination of the earth in the precise and fastest way.
